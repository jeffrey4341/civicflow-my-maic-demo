## Who Needs a Licence

Anyone operating a business within the council area needs a council business
licence (lesen perniagaan). This includes food stalls and hawkers
(gerai makanan / penjaja, 小食档 / 摊位), retail shops, and home-based businesses
that receive customers. Keywords: licence, lesen, permit, business, perniagaan,
food stall, gerai, hawker, penjaja, 执照, 营业, 小食档.

## Food Stall / Hawker Licence — Documents Required

To apply for a food-stall or hawker licence, an applicant typically submits:

- A copy of identity document (NRIC) — masked in this demo.
- A passport-size photo.
- A typhoid vaccination certificate for food handlers.
- Proof of premises or the proposed stall location (tapak).
- Completed application form (Borang Lesen Penjaja).

## Information the Council Needs from You

Before the council can advise on or process a licence enquiry, it needs to know:
**the proposed business location**, **the business type** (e.g. cooked food,
drinks, packaged goods), and the **intended operating hours**. Missing any of
these will delay processing. This is the most common reason a licensing enquiry
cannot be answered immediately.

## Fees and Validity

Licence fees vary by business type and stall size (synthetic placeholder: RM30–RM240
per year in this demo). Licences are valid for one calendar year and are renewable.

## Processing Time (Service Charter)

Complete applications are processed within 14 working days. Incomplete enquiries
are returned to the applicant with a checklist of missing information.

## Department Routing

Business-licensing enquiries are routed to the **Licensing Department, Licensing
Unit**. Food premises may additionally be referred to the Health Unit for
inspection before a licence is granted.
