# Drainage Response SOP

**Document type:** Standard Operating Procedure (SYNTHETIC — demo only)
**Owner:** Engineering Department / Drainage Unit
**Reference:** SOP-DRN-001 (fictional)

> This is an invented procedure created for the CivicFlow MY Mobile demo. It does
> not represent any real council's standard operating procedure.

## Scope and Categories

This SOP covers citizen reports about drainage, blocked drains (longkang
tersumbat), monsoon drains, culverts, and surface flooding (banjir kilat / flash
flood). Reports mentioning rising water when it rains, overflowing drains, or
water entering premises fall under this SOP. Keywords: drain, drainage, longkang,
banjir, flood, hujan, air naik, tersumbat, parit.

## Urgency Classification

Drainage reports are triaged into three urgency levels:

- **Routine:** slow-draining or partially blocked drain with no flooding.
- **Urgent:** fully blocked drain in a residential or commercial area.
- **Flood-risk:** any report indicating water rising quickly during rain, water
  entering homes or shops, or a history of flash flooding at the location. A
  flood-risk classification is the highest priority.

## Flood-Risk Escalation

Any report classified as **flood-risk** must be escalated for **supervisor
approval** before a field crew is dispatched, because dispatch consumes limited
monsoon-season resources and may require coordination with the state drainage
authority. The escalation records the location, the reported water behaviour, and
any attached evidence. Field dispatch is never automatic — a human supervisor
authorises it.

## Response Targets (Service Charter)

- Flood-risk reports: acknowledge within 2 hours, supervisor decision within 4
  hours.
- Urgent blockages: site inspection within 24 hours.
- Routine drainage: site inspection within 5 working days.

## Information Required from Citizen

To act on a drainage report, officers need: the exact location or nearest
landmark, whether water has entered any building, the time the problem is worst
(e.g. during heavy rain), and a photo of the affected drain if available.

## Department Routing

Drainage cases are routed to the **Engineering Department, Drainage Unit**.
Cross-boundary monsoon drains may be co-referred to the state drainage authority,
but the council retains the citizen-facing case.
