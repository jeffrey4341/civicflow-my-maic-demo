CivicFlow MY Mobile uses AI as an assistive casework layer, not as an autonomous decision-maker. In the demo, AI is used for language detection, case classification, request summarisation, retrieval of synthetic SOP / FAQ / service-charter citations, routing recommendation, missing-information detection and citizen reply drafting. The system supports Malay, English, Chinese and Tamil citizen inputs and produces structured outputs that officers can review in the case console.

The artifact runs by default in deterministic local mode with no API key and no external model call. The deterministic TypeScript pipeline uses scripted language heuristics, category rules, policy retrieval over the bundled synthetic policy corpus and fixed reply templates. An optional Anthropic Claude path exists only if an ANTHROPIC_API_KEY is explicitly configured; it is not required for judging and is not used by the default demo path. Whether deterministic or LLM-assisted, the output schema remains the same.

Human oversight is mandatory. AI may draft, classify, cite and recommend, but it cannot close cases, approve high-risk escalations, dispatch field teams or decide eligibility for welfare, education aid, licensing or other public services. Flood-risk cases require supervisor approval before work can proceed. Missing-information cases remain blocked until required details are resolved. Low-confidence or zero-citation outputs fall back to manual review.

All data is synthetic and public-demo safe. The repository contains invented citizen cases, invented policy documents and placeholder identifiers only. It does not use real citizen data, real NRICs, real phone numbers, real addresses, real government SOPs, private agency records, production APIs or secrets. AI tools were used to assist development, writing, review and packaging, but the team remains responsible for the artifact, its code, its disclosures and its hackathon submission materials.
